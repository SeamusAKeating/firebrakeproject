    Things we learnt:

    Our existing structure works for most of our content, with no massive gaps. The content placed in in Using Registers doesn’t fit together as a group. Users grouped our content in five main groups:

  • Product: What is it, how does it work
  • Support: Help me, hopefully let me speak to a human.
  • Technical Documentation: How do I do technical things
  • Registers Pipeline: Things in the future, possibly technical.
  • A Single Register: Things about a specific register
  • Download and view were not seen as technical words, so were not associated with tech docs, despite being about technical things
  • Available registers was not seen as pipeline, which is where it currently sits, because they already exist

      • Things we learnt:

    • The concept of having a single authority source for data appealed to custodians
    • Custodians want more people to see their data, and were often told registers would become the place to go for government data.
    • This is partly about helping open up data and increasing transparency
    • The ability to link data increases the usefulness of that data.
    • The additional workload for custodians in terms of maintaining a register was seen as minimal, but the process of creating a register was a lot more labour intensive than anticipated.
    • The expectation was that you would just take the existing data and turn it into a register
    • The register had also not replaced lists as anticipated, due to fear the stripped back nature made it less useful to existing users of the data set.
    • Registers are not top priority for custodians but have to compete against demands of their day job.

    • Custodians valued meeting us face to face, as it built up rapport.
    • Custodians struggled to understand what exactly it was we were offering/ asking them to do, especially via email. This was due to the complexity of what we were offering, as well as it being hard to understand in the abstract.
    • Major concern around registers was around whether the data would be useful to existing users when it was turned into a register. They were not sure of the value of another set of data. They had a very clear idea of who the existing users were, and they felt Some saw the register audience as machines or developers
    • There was also a concern around blank data - what was the concerns? What is blank data?.
    • Lack of feedback - feedback to Custodians? on usage increased uncertainty around a value of registers. The perceived inflexibility of registers was also a concern for custodians.
    • Alpha and Beta were not meaningful labels for Custodians - why?
    • 'There is no way we can move towards the GDS one, because it is a lot worse than an excel spreadsheet for providing information to the members of the public” p3 - maybe explain here how/why we have to remove some data provided by custodians before creating a register
    • Quality of data was also a concern for custodians
    • For their existing audience, it was not seen as adding value
    • Registers are a nice to have, competing against day job
